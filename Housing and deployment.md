This project utilized a free sensor housing design. The components were a peanut butter container, a dole fruit cup, electrical tape, and cyanoacrylate superglue. 
First, a 1/2 inch hole was cut through the container and fruit cup for the sensor transducer to go into. 
Then, the two pieces were glued together and wrapped with electrical tape for extra waterproofing and support. 
Finally, the sensor was inserted into the housing and deployed. 

# Deploying 
Deployed at CMS dock on 4-17 @3pm at low tide ( -6inches known tide )

After housing was finished, a mounting platform was created with an 8" section of 1x4 pressure treated lumber and 4 small eye hooks. 
The sensor housing was then zip tied into the eye hooks and the sensor was screwed to the dock that the sensor was mounted on.

Important considerations for mounting include being able to return the sensor to the same place on the dock with semi-permanent mounting hardware, as if the sensor must be removed for cleaning or maintenence, it can be returned to the same location. 
The sensor can be surveyed in with GPS and connected to a datum if necessary, or deployed as is.
